## 1. Purpose

D3's corruption mechanisms are synthetic constructs. D5 exists to check that at least one of them — concept shift, D1 §7.3 — produces degradation _comparable in kind and magnitude_ to genuine, naturally-occurring shift, not just a mathematically well-formed but potentially unrealistic manipulation. D1 §7.3 names two specific anchor datasets for this purpose: `road-safety` and `diabetes_readmission`.

**Correction from D11 (locked in for this document):** D5 was originally scoped around a ΔAUC-based comparison. D11 Decision 1 established **Relative ΔM** — `(M_clean − M_corrupted)/M_clean` — as the project's actual prediction target. D5 uses Relative ΔM throughout, not ΔAUC, for consistency with every other deliverable.

## 2. Investigation: What Natural Split Actually Exists

Before choosing a methodology, the actual processed corpus was inspected directly (`find_natural_shift_candidates.py`) rather than assuming a multi-year temporal split existed, as D1's framing might suggest.

**Finding 1 — road-safety is NOT multi-year in this processed corpus.** `Date` spans exactly 365 unique days, entirely within 2015. A year-over-year split, which might have been the first assumption, is not available. Real alternatives found: a within-year temporal split (e.g., first half vs. second half of 2015), a geographic split (`Police_Force`, 49 regions), or an Urban vs. Rural split (`Urban_or_Rural_Area`, clean binary, 27,001 / 17,170 rows).

**Finding 2 — diabetes_readmission has no temporal field at all** in this processed dataset — confirmed, not assumed. `time_in_hospital` superficially matched a "temporal" keyword search but is per-visit duration, not a cohort/period variable, and was correctly rejected. Real alternatives found among the remaining columns: `admission_source_id`, `medical_specialty`, `payer_code`.

**Finding 3, incidental — a shared missingness block in road-safety.** `Police_Force`, `Date`, `Day_of_Week`, `Time`, `Urban_or_Rural_Area`, and `Did_Police_Officer_Attend_Scene_of_Accident` all show nearly identical missing-row counts (5,829–5,837), strongly suggesting a single administrative subset of ~5,830 records is missing all of this metadata together, not independent per-column missingness. Noted here as a citable data-quality observation; not acted on further in this document.

## 3. Decisions

**Decision 1 — road-safety's natural split: Urban vs. Rural (`Urban_or_Rural_Area`).** Chosen for simplicity over the two alternatives: it's already a clean, pre-computed binary field, unlike the temporal option (which requires picking a specific, somewhat arbitrary cutoff date) or the geographic option (which requires designing a defensible grouping across 49 police-force categories). Both resulting populations are large (27,001 Urban / 17,170 Rural) — no small-sample concerns.

**Decision 2 — diabetes_readmission's natural split: `admission_source_id`, binarized into Emergency vs. Non-emergency admission.** Chosen over `medical_specialty` and `payer_code` because this specific binarization is a well-established simplification already used in the wider diabetes-readmission literature for this exact dataset, whereas the other two candidates are known to carry substantial "Unknown/Invalid" concentration in this dataset's real distribution, requiring cleanup work the simpler option avoids.

**Decision 3 — direction of comparison: train on the larger population, evaluate on the smaller. Confirmed against real data:**

- **road-safety:** train on Urban (27,001 rows), evaluate on Rural (17,170 rows). 5,829 rows have missing `Urban_or_Rural_Area` (the same shared administrative-metadata gap noted in §2, Finding 3) and are excluded from both groups.
- **diabetes_readmission:** train on Emergency admissions (28,245 rows), evaluate on Non-emergency (18,280 rows). 3,475 rows fall under ambiguous/unknown admission-source codes and are excluded from both groups.

**A confirming signal for both splits, found while checking group sizes:** target base rates are nearly identical between each split's two groups — `Sex_of_Driver` prevalence is 0.3043 (Urban) vs. 0.3040 (Rural); readmission rate is 0.1166 (Emergency) vs. 0.1051 (Non-emergency). This is a good property for concept-shift validation specifically: since the label's marginal distribution barely shifts between groups, any measured Relative ΔM will primarily reflect a genuine change in the feature-label _relationship_ (concept shift), not a confound from the label's overall rate moving (which would look more like label shift). Both chosen splits are well-suited to what D5 is actually trying to validate.

**Decision 4 — all four model families evaluated, matching the rest of the project's convention.** No simplification here: D1/D3/D4 already fix XGBoost, Random Forest, Logistic Regression, and MLP as the evaluated family set, and D5's comparison should use the same four rather than a subset, so its results are directly comparable to Phase 2's meta-modeling results later.

## 4. Methodology

For each anchor dataset, with its natural split into Population A (train) and Population B (natural-shift eval):

1. Split Population A itself into a train/held-out-test partition (D2's stratified-split convention, same seed/ratio conventions already used elsewhere).
2. Train each of the four model families on Population A's train partition.
3. Evaluate each trained model on:
   - Population A's held-out test partition → **M_clean** (in-distribution baseline).
   - Population B, in full → **M_shifted** (natural-shift evaluation).
4. Compute **Relative ΔM = (M_clean − M_shifted) / M_clean** per model family, per anchor dataset. This gives four real, measured natural-shift degradation values per dataset (one per model family).
5. Separately, run D3's `distribution_shift.py` concept-shift mechanism against the same dataset across D4's real severity grid, computing the same Relative ΔM at each severity level.
6. Compare: identify which synthetic severity level's Relative ΔM most closely matches the real, natural-shift Relative ΔM from step 4. This comparison is what allows D1 §7.3 to treat these two datasets as concept shift's severity anchor — if no severity level in D4's grid produces a comparable magnitude, that's a finding requiring D4's concept-shift `α_max` to be revisited for these two datasets specifically.

## 5. Final Real Results and Interpretation

The pipeline (`d5_natural_shift_pipeline.py`) was run against the real corpus after fixing two implementation bugs found on first contact with real data: (1) one-hot-encoded column names containing brackets — e.g. `diabetes_readmission`'s `age`, binned as string ranges like `[70-80)` — crashed XGBoost entirely; fixed by sanitizing all encoded column names. (2) `StandardScaler` was imported but never applied, leaving scale-sensitive families (logistic regression, MLP) undertrained relative to tree-based families — confirmed by road-safety's MLP clean AUC jumping from 0.5607 (barely above chance) to 0.7680 once fixed.

**Real natural-shift Relative ΔM (averaged across the four families):**

- road-safety (Urban → Rural): **+0.0141**. Per-family range: LR −0.0077 (a small, direction-reversing outlier — the model trained on Urban performed marginally _better_ on Rural than on its own held-out Urban test set), RF +0.0277, MLP +0.0304, XGBoost +0.0061.
- diabetes_readmission (Emergency → Non-emergency): **+0.0335**. Per-family range: LR +0.0290, RF +0.0355, MLP +0.0567, XGBoost +0.0130 — all positive this time, more internally consistent than road-safety.

**Best-matching synthetic severity level:**

- road-safety: severity=0.050 (gap=0.0021) — the _lowest_ level in D4's 10-level grid.
- diabetes_readmission: severity=0.100 (gap=0.0092) — the second-lowest level.
- In both cases, the gap grows **monotonically** as severity increases beyond the best match.

**Interpretation, not just "good match, done":**

1. **D4's low-severity concept-shift calibration is validated as realistic for both anchor datasets.** Real, naturally-occurring concept shift genuinely sits at the gentle end of D4's severity range.
2. **D4's higher severities (roughly 0.15 and above) are not validated — or invalidated — by this exercise.** Neither anchor dataset naturally exhibits shift that large, so D5 has nothing to say about whether those levels resemble real-world conditions or are a deliberate stress-test beyond natural bounds. This is a genuine scope limit of natural-shift validation, not a weakness in the higher severities themselves — stated explicitly here so it isn't silently assumed either way.
3. **The monotonic gap growth suggests the true natural-shift magnitude may sit below 0.05**, the current grid's lowest tested level — the match might tighten further with a finer-grained low end, not currently available in D4's 10-level grid.
4. **Not every family agrees in direction, let alone magnitude.** road-safety's logistic regression showing a small negative Relative ΔM means the "average across families" is a genuine summary, but it blends real per-family disagreement, not a single consistent signal.

**Conclusion: D4's concept-shift calibration is supported at the low end for both anchor datasets, with the explicit caveat that this says nothing about the grid's upper range.** No revision to D4 is required based on this finding — the low-severity anchor holds — but D4's documentation should note that severities above ~0.10–0.15 for concept shift are stress-test conditions without a confirmed natural analogue in this corpus, not claimed-realistic degradation levels.

## 6. Future Work (Explicitly Deferred, Not Forgotten)

1. **road-safety's temporal alternative.** A within-year seasonal split (e.g., winter vs. summer months) would capture a different, arguably more universally-recognized notion of "natural shift" than Urban/Rural — worth revisiting if Urban/Rural's comparison turns out to be a poor anchor for concept shift's severity grid.
2. **road-safety's geographic alternative.** `Police_Force`'s 49 regions could support a richer, multi-population comparison (not just one binary split) — deferred due to the added complexity of choosing a defensible train/eval regional grouping.
3. **diabetes_readmission's richer alternatives.** `medical_specialty` and `payer_code`, despite their missingness issues, could still yield a usable split after targeted cleanup (e.g., grouping rare specialties into an "Other" bucket) — deferred as extra preprocessing work not justified for this version.
4. **Symmetric (both-directions) comparison.** Training on the smaller population and evaluating on the larger, in addition to the current one-directional design, would give a fuller picture of whether the natural shift's effect is asymmetric — deferred for simplicity.
5. **The shared missingness block in road-safety** (§2, Finding 3) is a real, citable data-quality finding not yet written up anywhere formal — worth adding to the project's accumulated findings alongside HELOC's sentinels and bank-marketing's `pdays` convention if this level of finding is being tracked for the paper.
